Research

Banqiao Veterans Home - Project Context

Target Facility: A long-term care center specifically serving veterans who came to Taiwan with the KMT government, providing specialized care for elderly residents with dementia.

Staffing Challenge

  • 5 nurses - Each caring for 20-24 residents (100 beds total)
  • 16 care assistants - Each caring for 5-6 residents
  • Multiple rooms per floor with limited supervision

Current Solutions

  • Listening Station - Music therapy area
  • Virtual Train Station - Nostalgic experience
  • Art Classes - Calligraphy, painting workshops
  • Board Games - Chess and traditional games

Core Dilemma

Safety vs. Freedom: Staff must restrict resident movement to ensure safety, but this limitation negatively impacts residents' emotional well-being and increases care difficulty. The challenge is enabling safe free movement while maintaining real-time location awareness.

Taiwan's Aging Crisis

As Taiwan rapidly enters a super-aged society, long-term care facilities face unprecedented challenges. By 2025, approximately 290,000 people will have dementia, yet medical resources cannot accommodate everyone in institutional care, creating enormous demand for care innovation.

Motivation for Innovation

  • 2025: Taiwan entering super-aged society
  • ~290,000 people projected to have dementia
  • Limited institutional capacity - Not all can enter care facilities
  • Growing demand for specialized dementia care solutions

Research Methodology

1. Contextual Inquiry (On-site Observation)

Why: Understanding real-world context

  • Observed care staff workflows in actual environment
  • Identified unspoken pain points and workarounds
  • Captured non-verbal behaviors and environmental constraints
  • Observed resident-staff interactions during care routines

When to use: Early-stage research to understand real user behaviors, not just what they say they do. Essential for healthcare settings with complex workflows.

2. In-depth Interviews

Why: Capturing user motivations and pain points

  • Conducted 15+ interviews with nursing staff and caregivers
  • Asked open-ended questions about daily challenges
  • Understood emotional context (stress, safety concerns)
  • Identified hidden needs not visible in observation

When to use: Best for exploring "why" behind behaviors. Qualitative data reveals motivations, concerns, and workarounds that observation alone cannot capture.

3. Surveys & Questionnaires

Why: Gathering quantifiable data from larger sample

  • Distributed to all 21 care staff members (nurses + assistants)
  • Measured frequency of specific pain points
  • Validated hypotheses from interviews with statistical backing
  • Prioritized problems by severity and frequency

When to use: Efficient for quantifying patterns across larger populations. Validates qualitative findings and reveals statistically significant issues.

4. Usability Testing (Iterative)

Why: Validating design solutions with actual users

  • Tested low-fidelity and high-fidelity prototypes with care staff
  • Conducted task-based testing (e.g., "Check resident location in 10 seconds")
  • Measured task success rate and time-on-task
  • Identified usability issues before full implementation

When to use: Critical for iterating on designs. Reveals how users actually interact with solutions and what needs adjustment before development.
