Problem

Less than half of U.S. adults (47%) reach the recommended weekly physical movement levels.

This statistic comes from CDC surveillance data based on the Physical Activity Guidelines for Americans, which track the percentage of adults meeting recommended aerobic and muscle-strengthening activity levels.

Solutions
Whitepaper Research

Starting with white paper research, I began to draw from research articlaes on the topic of Physical Activity Adherence, Motivation & Behavior Change... when I stumbled upon several statistics from JMIR Publication:

“...one-size-fits-all physical activity programs did not fit their lifestyles and reduced their willingness to continue using the app.”

“... uncertain about what types and amounts of physical activity were sufficient, often underestimating the value of everyday movement.”

“... Motivation to engage in physical activity declines rapidly when feedback is delayed and progress is not immediately visible.”

  1. Tran, J. et al. Engagement Features in Physical Activity Smartphone Apps: Focus Group Study With Sedentary People. JMIR mHealth and uHealth, 2019.
  2. Künsting, J. et al. Exploring Users’ Experiences of the Uptake and Adoption of Physical Activity Apps. JMIR mHealth and uHealth, 2018.
  3. Various Authors. Systematic review on gamification and feedback effectiveness in physical activity interventions. BMC Public Health / JMIR Publications.
Research Sythesize

We have summarized the literature reviews into three main reasons:

Persona

Sam is a busy retail worker in his 30s who wants to build consistent workout habits but struggles with time, motivation, and generic fitness advice.

competitive audits

We analyzed leading fitness apps including Strava, Fitbit, Nike Run Club and Apple Fitness.

Most fitness apps focus heavily on tracking but offer NO personalized based on users’ fitness level, health literacy, or real-life constraints—creating a high barrier for beginners and underserved users.

Design Direction

Based on the three main research insights that we sythesized, we did more literature reviews to figure out how to solve these three main pain points.

The Style Guide

According to color psychology, the purple (Lavendar) color series can soothe the user's emotions and provide gentle stimulation.
