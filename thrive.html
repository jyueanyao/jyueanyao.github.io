Problem

Less than half of American adults complete recommended weekly activity.

Sedentary routines compound cardiovascular risk, even for communities that understand the stakes.

CDC data shows sustained inactivity keeps people on the path toward preventable disease. Interviews confirmed that even motivated adults rarely turn numbers into action when daily life gets in the way.

Solutions
Research

Behavior science and interviews revealed three blockers to lasting change.

We combined literature review, competitive audits, and community interviews to understand why motivation collapses after the early wins.

1. Lack of motivation

Motivation dropped after week two whenever Thrive felt like a generic tracker instead of a coach.

Reason 02: Literacy gaps stall progress

Limited health literacy made metrics feel abstract; participants asked for concrete translation into daily movement.

Reason 03: Rigid plans break in real life

Schedules, energy, and physical limitations change constantly, leaving one-size plans abandoned.

Insight

Accountability partners and adaptive coaching keep momentum alive.

When people see how small actions stack into heart-health impact, confidence and follow-through rise.

Behavior change sticks when guidance is adaptive, affirming, and framed around achievable wins instead of perfection.

Design Question

How might we turn clinical guidance into repeatable, rewarding routines?

This question anchored every iteration to avoid feature creep and keep the product grounded in evidence.

Iterations

From novelty-driven gamification to sustained behavior change.

Version 1 - Flashy but fleeting. Early prototypes leaned on streaks and badges, but motivation tanked after the honeymoon period.

Version 2 - Guided retention loops. We rebuilt reinforcement schedules, layered contextual coaching, and introduced social accountability to extend engagement.

Final Experience

A cohesive companion for daily heart health.

  • Personalized guidance updates with every check-in and movement log.
  • Health overviews connect sleep, vitals, and activity into one story.
  • Reward systems reinforce consistent effort instead of perfection.
  • Community journeys transform progress into a shared experience.
Deliverables

Everything packaged for stakeholder review.

High-fidelity prototypes, a behavioral design style guide, and workshop-ready storytelling decks support the next validation round.

Reflection

Evidence-based design turns guidelines into lived routines.

Thrive reinforced that health products succeed when they respect motivation, accessibility, and sustainability, not just data accuracy.

Grounding every decision in research showed stakeholders how UX strategy translates public health insight into daily behavior change.
